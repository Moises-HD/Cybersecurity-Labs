# ⚖️ Cybersecurity Regulations & Compliance — Cybersecurity Master's Program

This module focuses on regulatory compliance, corporate ethics, and legal frameworks applicable to cybersecurity.

It includes the study of national and international standards, ISO management systems, the NIS Directive, and data protection regulations, along with practical application of GDPR, ENS, and anti-bribery compliance policies.

---

## 🧠 Lab Overview

These exercises demonstrate how organizations align cybersecurity practices with legal, regulatory, and ethical requirements, integrating governance, risk management, and compliance (GRC) principles.

---

## 📂 Lab Summary

### 🧩 Corporate Governance & Business Ethics

Evaluation of corporate ethics and governance principles within a fictional company (**ACME**), integrating:

- Information security  
- Environmental sustainability  
- Workplace equality  

**Key commitments identified:**
- Transparency with clients and employees  
- Personal data protection aligned with ISO 27001  
- Responsible energy usage (solar panels, recycling)  
- Prevention of bribery, discrimination, and unethical practices  

**Third-party due diligence:**
- Ethical clauses in contracts  
- Periodic audits  
- Reputation and risk checks  

---

### ⚙️ Regulatory Compliance & Risk Management

Identification of key regulations applicable to the technology sector:

- GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)  
- Telecommunications Law  
- Anti-Money Laundering regulations  

**Compliance risk analysis:**
- ISO 27001 risk → loss of certification due to security failures  
- GDPR risk → data exposure or misuse  
- Environmental risk → non-compliance with sustainability commitments  

**Mitigation controls:**
- Regular security audits  
- Updated privacy policies  
- Energy efficiency monitoring  

**Compliance metrics defined:**
- Employee training rates  
- Incident reduction  
- Audit coverage  
- Customer satisfaction  

---

### 🕵️ Criminal Compliance & Anti-Bribery

Development of a criminal risk matrix for a technology company:

- Cybercrime, financial and environmental offenses  
- Money laundering, corruption, bribery, fraud  
- Privacy and market-related violations  

**Mitigation strategies:**
- External financial audits  
- Whistleblowing channels  
- Ethics and compliance training  
- Internal controls for detecting irregular payments  
- Monitoring high-risk clients  

**Anti-bribery scenarios analyzed:**
- International licensing  
- Public procurement  
- High-corruption environments  

---

### 🔒 Data Protection & GDPR

Identification of key data protection principles:

- Confidentiality, integrity, availability  
- Data minimization, lawfulness, transparency, purpose limitation  

**Types of data processed:**
- Personal data (name, ID, banking data, IP, geolocation, etc.)  
- Sensitive data (e.g., financial or location data)  

**Legal basis for processing:**
- Contractual necessity  
- Legal obligation  
- Explicit consent  
- Legitimate interest  

**Impact assessments (PIA/DPIA):**
- Billing systems → encryption and access control  
- Call logs → minimization and traceability  
- Marketing → consent and opt-out mechanisms  

---

### 🌐 International Standards & ENS Implementation

Study and application of major cybersecurity frameworks:

#### 🔹 NIST Framework

Implementation across the five pillars:

- **Identify:** Asset inventory and risk assessment  
- **Protect:** Access control, encryption, training  
- **Detect:** SIEM, IDS/IPS, log analysis  
- **Respond:** Incident response plans and secure communication  
- **Recover:** Backups and disaster recovery plans  

---

#### 🔹 ISO 27001 — Information Security

Definition of organizational context, stakeholders, and risks (attacks, leaks, failures).

**Controls implemented:**
- Firewalls and network segmentation  
- Multi-factor authentication  
- Redundancy and load balancing  

**Metrics:**
- MTTD (Mean Time to Detect)  
- MTTR (Mean Time to Respond)  
- Compliance indicators  

---

#### 🔹 ISO 22301 — Business Continuity

Impact analysis for service disruptions:

- Financial, reputational, and legal impact  

**Key parameters:**
- MTPD: 3 hours  
- RPO: 15 minutes  
- RTO: 1 hour  

**Strategies:**
- Redundant data centers  
- Disaster recovery plans  
- Simulation exercises  

---

#### 🔹 ENS (National Security Framework)

Applied in the context of services provided to law enforcement agencies:

- **Classification level:**  
  High (Availability & Integrity) / Very High (Confidentiality)  

**Security measures:**
- MFA authentication  
- End-to-end encryption  
- 24/7 SOC monitoring  
- Defined security roles
